# User Personas
## AI Job Matching & Skills Gap Planner

---

## Persona 1: Job Seeker / Candidate

### Name
Aarav Sharma

### Profile
- Age: 24
- Location: Mumbai
- Education: Bachelor’s degree
- Experience: 1–3 years
- Goal: Find a relevant job quickly and understand how to become more employable
- Digital comfort: Moderate
- Pain tolerance for complexity: Low

### Background
Aarav has completed his education and has some internship or early-career experience. He is actively searching for jobs but is frustrated by generic job portals and unclear job requirements. He often applies to roles without knowing whether he is actually a fit.

### Goals
- Understand which jobs are relevant to his profile
- Know why he matches a role
- Identify which skills he is missing
- Get a simple learning path to improve fit
- Reduce wasted time applying blindly

### Pain Points
- Job descriptions are vague or overloaded with buzzwords
- No transparency on why a role is recommended
- Unsure which missing skills actually matter
- Too many irrelevant job listings
- No practical guidance on what to learn next

### Needs from the Product
- Simple profile entry or CV upload
- Clear top job recommendations
- Match score that feels credible
- Transparent explanation of match
- Actionable skill-gap and learning recommendations

### Success Criteria
Aarav should leave the product with:
- 3–5 relevant target roles
- a clear understanding of his strongest fit
- a shortlist of missing skills
- a realistic next-step learning plan

## Persona 2: Mid-Career Reskilling Candidate

### Name
Fatima Khan

### Profile
- Age: 35
- Location: Dubai
- Education: Master’s degree
- Experience: 8+ years
- Goal: Shift into a new role or adjacent function
- Digital comfort: Moderate to high
- Pain tolerance for complexity: Medium

### Background
Fatima has solid work experience but wants to move into a different function due to market changes, role stagnation, or personal growth goals. She has transferable skills, but she is not sure how those map to new jobs.

### Goals
- Understand adjacent job opportunities
- Translate existing skills into new career paths
- Identify realistic gaps rather than start from zero
- Build a targeted reskilling plan
- Avoid wasting time on courses with low value

### Pain Points
- Existing job platforms do not explain transferable fit well
- Hard to tell which missing skills are critical versus optional
- Reskilling options are too broad and unprioritized
- Lack of confidence in career transition decisions

### Needs from the Product
- Explainable matching beyond exact keyword overlap
- Visibility into transferable skills
- Targeted learning recommendations
- Confidence that the next step is practical and not random

### Success Criteria
Fatima should be able to:
- identify viable adjacent roles
- understand what makes her a partial fit
- prioritize high-impact skills to close the gap
- build a short reskilling roadmap

## Persona 3: Workforce Program Admin

### Name
Rahul Mehta

### Profile
- Age: 39
- Role: Program Manager / Operations Lead
- Organization: Government workforce mission / skilling agency / employment support program
- Goal: Improve candidate outcomes and monitor program efficiency
- Digital comfort: High
- Decision style: Data-driven

### Background
Rahul oversees candidate intake, matching outcomes, training interventions, and reporting to leadership or funders. He is less interested in model sophistication and more interested in measurable outcomes and operational control.

### Goals
- Track candidate flow through the placement funnel
- Identify common missing skills across cohorts
- Understand which job categories are in demand
- Improve conversion from match to application to placement
- Use evidence to refine training interventions

### Pain Points
- Limited visibility into where candidates drop off
- No unified view of skill gaps across applicants
- Difficulty linking training efforts to job outcomes
- Static reporting that does not support action
- Too much anecdotal decision-making

### Needs from the Product
- Clear operational dashboard
- Funnel visibility by stage
- Skill-gap trends that support intervention planning
- Ability to filter by location or job category
- Decision-ready metrics, not just decorative charts

### Success Criteria
Rahul should be able to:
- identify where the funnel is weak
- see which skill gaps are most common
- understand which candidate segments need support
- report program performance clearly to stakeholders

## Persona 4: Policy / Program Stakeholder

### Name
Nadia Fernandes

### Profile
- Age: 46
- Role: Policy Lead / Sponsor / Program Director
- Goal: Ensure program is delivering measurable employment value
- Digital comfort: Medium
- Time availability: Low

### Background
Nadia does not use the product daily, but she needs confidence that the program is producing meaningful outcomes. She wants evidence that candidate guidance and training support are aligned with labor-market opportunities.

### Goals
- Evaluate whether the program is improving employability
- See if training aligns with recurring skill gaps
- Assess whether matching quality supports placements
- Justify budget, program design, or expansion decisions

### Pain Points
- High-level reporting often lacks operational substance
- Hard to connect candidate support efforts to actual outcomes
- Too much technical explanation and not enough program insight

### Needs from the Product
- Clear KPI reporting
- Simple visuals showing outcomes and bottlenecks
- Evidence of where intervention is needed
- Confidence that the product supports both user value and program value

### Success Criteria
Nadia should be able to:
- understand program performance in minutes
- identify whether interventions are working
- support decisions with credible metrics
- communicate impact to leadership or external stakeholders

## Persona Prioritization

### Primary Persona
**Job Seeker / Candidate**  
The product fails if candidate recommendations are not useful, clear, and actionable.

### Secondary Persona
**Workforce Program Admin**  
The product also fails if admin users cannot monitor performance and act on skill-gap data.

### Tertiary Personas
- Mid-Career Reskilling Candidate
- Policy / Program Stakeholder

These personas matter, but the MVP should first optimize for candidate value and admin visibility.

---

## Key Product Implications from Personas

### For Candidates
The interface must be:
- simple
- transparent
- actionable
- low-friction

### For Admins
The dashboard must be:
- operational
- measurable
- filterable
- decision-oriented

### For Stakeholders
The reporting must be:
- concise
- outcome-focused
- credible
- easy to interpret

---

## Summary
These personas show that the product serves two core layers:

1. **individual guidance**
   - help candidates find relevant jobs and close skill gaps

2. **program intelligence**
   - help admins and stakeholders improve employment outcomes at scale

That dual value is the whole point of the product.
